Homemade Pho

This recipe keeps the traditional depth of pho while still being simple enough to make at home. Most of the time is for simmering the broth (1.5–2 hours).

Ingredients
Broth Base
  • 1.5 to 2 lbs beef bones (marrow or knuckle) — or chicken carcass for chicken pho
  • 1 medium onion, halved
  • 3 to 4 inch piece of ginger, halved lengthwise
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds (optional)
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tsp sugar (rock sugar preferred, white sugar works)
  • 8 cups water
Noodles & Toppings
  • 12 oz dried rice noodles (banh pho)
  • 8 to 10 oz beef (sirloin, flank, or brisket) — thinly sliced, slightly frozen for easier cutting
  • Fresh bean sprouts
  • Fresh Thai basil
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges
  • Sliced chili peppers
  • Hoisin sauce & sriracha (optional)
Instructions
  1. Prepare the bones (for a clear broth):

    Place bones in a pot, cover with cold water. Bring to a boil for 10 minutes to release impurities. Drain, rinse bones under cold water, and clean the pot.

  2. Char onion & ginger:

    Char directly over a gas flame or under the oven broiler until slightly blackened (5–7 minutes). Peel away loose burnt skin.

  3. Simmer the broth:

    Return bones to the clean pot with ~8 cups water. Add charred onion, ginger, star anise, cinnamon, cloves, and coriander seeds. Simmer gently (do not boil hard) for 1.5–2 hours, skimming foam as needed.

  4. Strain & season:

    Strain out bones and spices, leaving a clear broth. Season with fish sauce, sugar, and salt — taste and adjust as needed.

  5. Cook the noodles:

    Soak dried rice noodles in warm water (per package directions), then boil 2–3 minutes until tender. Drain and divide among 4 bowls.

  6. Assemble & serve hot:

    Arrange raw beef slices over noodles. Bring broth back to a full boil, then pour directly into bowls — the hot broth cooks the beef instantly. Add sprouts, herbs, lime, chili, and sauces as desired.

Tips:
  • Freeze beef for 20 to 30 minutes before slicing to cut thin slices easily.
  • Rock sugar gives a cleaner, more natural sweetness; white sugar works in a pinch.
  • Keep broth at a gentle simmer, not a rolling boil, for a clear and clean flavor.

Com Tam (Vietnamese Broken Rice)

A classic Vietnamese dish of broken rice served with grilled pork chops, shredded pork skin, egg meatloaf, pickled vegetables, and fish sauce dressing.

Ingredients
For the Rice
  • 2 cups broken rice (or substitute jasmine rice)
  • Water for steaming
For Grilled Pork Chops
  • 4 pork chops, thin cut
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp lemongrass, minced
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp black pepper
For Egg Meatloaf
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp minced onion

Instructions

  1. Cook the rice:

    Steam broken rice (or jasmine) as usual. Keep warm.

  2. Marinate pork chops:

    Mix fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, garlic, lemongrass, oil, and pepper. Marinate pork for at least 1 hour (overnight best).

  3. Grill pork chops:

    Grill on charcoal or pan until golden and cooked through (about 4–5 minutes per side).

  4. Make egg meatloaf:

    Beat eggs with fish sauce, sugar, onion. Steam in a heatproof dish until set (about 20 min).

  5. Prepare sides:

    Make quick pickled carrots & daikon. Slice cucumbers and tomatoes. Warm shredded pork skin. Prepare scallion oil by heating oil, then pouring over chopped scallions with a pinch of salt.

  6. Fish sauce dressing:

    Mix 3 tbsp fish sauce, 3 tbsp water, 2 tbsp sugar, juice of ½ lime, 1 clove garlic (minced), and sliced chili. Stir until sugar dissolves.

  7. Assemble:

    On each plate: scoop rice, add grilled pork chop, a slice of egg meatloaf, shredded pork skin, pickles, cucumber, and tomato. Drizzle with scallion oil and serve with fish sauce on the side.

Tips:
  • Charcoal grilling gives the best flavor for pork chops.
  • Make extra fish sauce — guests will want more for dipping.
  • You can swap pork chops with grilled chicken or beef for variety.

Banh Xeo (Vietnamese Crispy Savory Pancake)

A golden, crispy rice flour pancake stuffed with shrimp, pork, bean sprouts, and herbs. Served with fresh vegetables and fish sauce for dipping.

Ingredients
For the Batter
  • 2 cups broken rice (or substitute jasmine rice)
  • Water for steaming
For Grilled Pork Chops
  • 1 cup rice flour
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1½ cups cold water
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 scallions, thinly sliced
For the Filling
  • 200g (7 oz) pork belly or pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 200g (7 oz) shrimp, peeled & deveined
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • ½ onion, thinly sliced
  • Cooking oil (enough to coat the pan generously)
For Serving
  • Fresh lettuce leaves
  • Herbs: mint, basil, perilla, cilantro
  • Pickled carrots & daikon (optional)
  • Vietnamese dipping sauce

Instructions

  1. Prepare batter:

    Mix rice flour, cornstarch, turmeric, salt, water, and coconut milk until smooth. Add scallions. Let rest 30 minutes.

  2. Cook filling:

    In a hot skillet with a little oil, quickly stir-fry sliced pork and shrimp with onion until just cooked. Remove from pan.

  3. Make pancake:

    Add 1–2 tbsp oil to the pan. Pour in a thin layer of batter, swirling to coat. Add a few pieces of pork, shrimp, and a handful of bean sprouts on one side.

  4. Crisp & fold:

    Cover the pan with a lid for 1–2 minutes to steam the filling. Uncover, let the pancake crisp until edges are golden and crunchy. Fold in half like a taco.

  5. Serve:

    Place pancake on a plate. Serve with lettuce, herbs, and dipping sauce. To eat, cut a piece, wrap in lettuce + herbs, and dip into fish sauce.

Tips:
  • Keep the batter thin for extra crispy pancakes.
  • Use a non-stick skillet and plenty of oil for best results.
  • Eat immediately while hot and crunchy!
